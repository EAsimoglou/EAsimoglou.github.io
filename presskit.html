Jemima Abu is a self-taught Front End Developer and school-taught Systems Engineer from Lagos, Nigeria. She currently works at Telesoftas, a software company based in Lithuania. She is an avid advocate for diversity and intersectionality in technology and accessibility in web development.

She is actively involved in the tech community and has spoken at over twenty international conferences such as React Summit and the International JavaScript Conference, to audiences ranging from 20 to 20,000 people.

She writes articles targeted towards helping beginners get into tech and has had her works published on highly rated online magazines including freeCodeCamp, CSSTricks and EnvatoTuts.

She has previously volunteered at organizations like Pearls Africa, teaching the basics of technology to girls from less privileged environments and GirlCode Academy as a tutor to women looking to switch career paths into technology.

In her spare time, she likes to watch slice-of-life anime and cheesy Bollywood romcoms, read whatever form of literature she can get her hands on, light scented candles and carry out very elaborate skin care routines.
